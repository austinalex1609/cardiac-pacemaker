Dual Chambered Pacemaker

Dual chambered pacemakers stimulate both the atrium and the ventricle, closely mimicking the heart's natural pacing. The block diagram below illustrates the internal components and flow of signals within a dual chambered pacemaker.

Biventricular Pacemaker

A biventricular pacemaker, also called a cardiac resynchronization therapy (CRT) device, uses three leads placed in:

  • 1. Right atrium
  • 2. Right ventricle
  • 3. Left ventricle (via the coronary sinus vein)
